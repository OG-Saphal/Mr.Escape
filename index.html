Mr. Escape

A fast-paced elemental endless runner focused on survival, quick decision-making, and system-driven gameplay.

Prototype / Unfinished Nov 2019 – Apr 2020

1. Game Overview

Mr. Escape is a fast-paced endless runner where the player survives an ever-changing environment by switching characters and bullets based on elemental rules.

The game combines automatic forward movement, combat-driven survival, and a strategic element-based system that rewards awareness and quick reactions.

Note : Please ignore the messy design and the rainbow UI; my 15-year-old self thought this was peak aesthetic. 😂

2. Core Gameplay Pillars

Endless Survival

  • The game continues indefinitely until the player fails
  • Difficulty increases naturally over time

Element Awareness

  • Correct character and bullet selection is mandatory
  • Wrong choices lead to continuous damage or ineffective combat

Fast Decision Making

  • Quick character and bullet switching under pressure
  • Constant environmental and enemy threats

System-Driven Gameplay

  • All mechanics follow strict, readable rules
  • Player, enemies, and environments interact through systems

3. Core Objective & Fail Conditions

Primary Objective

Survive for as long as possible while completing missions during the run.

Fail Conditions

  • Player health reaches zero
  • Player falls off the platform
  • Player is killed by enemies

4. Player Mechanics

Movement

  • Automatic forward movement
  • Manual left/right movement using mobile accelerometer
  • Jump to avoid obstacles and hazards

Character Switching (Element System)

The player must switch characters based on the current environment to avoid continuous damage.

Switching Input (Left Joystick):

  • Top Right → Fire
  • Top Left → Water
  • Bottom Right → Holy (Pink)
  • Bottom Left → Poison (Green)

Environment Interaction Rule

  • Wrong character → continuous damage
  • Correct character → safe movement

Special Character – King Character

  • Can run on any platform without taking damage
  • Activate only for a limited duration

5. Combat System

Weapons

  • Bullets
  • Lasers
  • Bombs

Bullet Switching (Right Joystick)

  • Top Right → Fire Bullet
  • Top Left → Water Bullet
  • Bottom Right → Holy Bullet
  • Bottom Left → Poison Bullet

Shooting Mechanics

  • Fire button triggers attack
  • Automatic targeting of nearest enemy
  • Player can shoot while moving

Element-Based Damage Rules

  • Fire enemies cannot be damaged by fire bullets
  • Water enemies cannot be damaged by water bullets
  • Poision enemies cannot be damaged by Poision bullets
  • Holy enemies cannot be damaged by Holy bullets
  • Correct bullet selection is mandatory

6. Enemies & AI

Enemy Types

  • Stationary turrets
  • Laser shooters
  • Projectile shooters
  • Moving enemies

Enemy Attacks

  • Bullets
  • Lasers
  • Bombs
  • Projectiles

AI Complexity

  • Simple, readable attack patterns
  • Difficulty increases over time
  • Higher spawn rates and faster attacks
  • Multiple enemy types combined

7. Environment System

Environment Types

  • Fire / Lava
  • Water
  • Poison
  • Holy

Rules

  • Incorrect character causes continuous damage
  • Environments force constant decision-making

8. Level Generation

  • Fully procedural endless level generation
  • Platform segments generated dynamically
  • Difficulty increases through time survived

9. Progression & Meta Systems

Progression

  • Mission-based progression
  • Missions completed during runs
  • Difficulty scales with survival time

Unlockables

  • Character skins
  • Bullet skins
  • Laser skins
  • Bomb skins

9. Economy & Monetization

Currencies

  • Multiple in-game currencies

Monetization Strategy

  • Cosmetic-focused monetization
  • No gameplay advantages through purchases
  • Fair and skill-based progression
  • Watch Ads to respawn.

11. Conclusion

Mr. Escape was developed during my early learning phase in Unity, with a primary focus on understanding and implementing gameplay systems rather than achieving visual polish. As a result, several aspects including graphics quality, visual effects, performance optimization, and overall architecture still have significant room for improvement. These limitations are acknowledged as part of the learning process.
